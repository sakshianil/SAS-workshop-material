# Protocol Summary: AOM-301

## Study design

A fictional randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase III study of
AOM-101 100 mg once daily for four weeks in adults with elevated blood
pressure. Sixty subjects are screened and approximately 56 are randomized 1:1.

## Objectives

- Primary: compare change from baseline in systolic blood pressure at Week 4.
- Safety: summarize treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs), serious adverse
  events, discontinuations, and vital signs.

## Visits and treatment

Screening is followed by randomization/baseline, Week 2, and Week 4. Treatment
starts on randomization day. Subjects may discontinue early.

## Populations

- Screened: signed consent and received a subject identifier.
- Randomized: assigned Active or Placebo.
- Safety: received at least one dose.
- Intent-to-treat: randomized subjects.

## Programming implications

Dates, treatment exposure, population flags, baseline selection, and
treatment-emergent logic must be derived consistently. A protocol describes the
study; the SAP turns objectives into analysis rules.
